## Why this matters

Southeast Asia is emerging as a key region for Open RAN adoption. Understanding the regional landscape—from policy drivers to deployment challenges—is essential for telecom professionals working in or with SEA markets.

</section>

<section class="lesson-pitfalls">

## Pitfalls

1. **Assuming homogeneity.** SEA is diverse. Singapore ≠ Indonesia ≠ Vietnam in maturity and approach.

2. **Ignoring geopolitics.** Vendor choices are political. US-China dynamics affect SEA decisions.

3. **Underestimating infrastructure.** Power and backhaul gaps affect Open RAN TCO assumptions.

4. **Overstating policy mandates.** Most SEA governments encourage, not mandate, Open RAN.

5. **Missing local context.** Operator relationships, spectrum allocations, and competitive dynamics vary.

6. **Skills gap blindness.** Open RAN requires different skills than traditional RAN. SEA has gaps.

7. **Scale economics assumptions.** SEA markets vary from tiny (Brunei) to massive (Indonesia).

8. **Timeline optimism.** Open RAN adoption in SEA is measured in years, not quarters.

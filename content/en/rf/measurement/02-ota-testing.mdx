<section class="lesson-hook">

## Why OTA testing?

Conducted measurements (cable-connected) miss the antenna. OTA testing measures the complete device — radio + antenna + enclosure — as it operates in real conditions. For devices with integrated antennas (smartphones, IoT, 5G radios), OTA testing is the only way to know true radiated performance.

Regulatory certification requires OTA test results.

</section>

<section class="lesson-intuition">

## Intuition

Cable testing is like measuring a speaker by connecting directly to an oscilloscope. OTA testing is like putting the speaker in a room and measuring what you actually hear. The room (chamber), your position (measurement angles), and measurement method all affect the result.

</section>

<section class="lesson-mechanism">

## Mechanism

### Key OTA metrics

**TRP (Total Radiated Power)**
$$\text{TRP} = \frac{1}{4\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^{\pi} [P_\theta(\theta,\phi) + P_\phi(\theta,\phi)] \sin\theta \, d\theta \, d\phi$$

Integrated radiated power over full sphere. Units: dBm.

**TIS (Total Isotropic Sensitivity)**
$$\text{TIS} = \frac{4\pi}{\int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^{\pi} \frac{\sin\theta}{S(\theta,\phi)} d\theta \, d\phi}$$

Average sensitivity over full sphere. Lower is better.

**EIRP (Effective Isotropic Radiated Power)**
$$\text{EIRP}(\theta,\phi) = P_t \cdot G(\theta,\phi)$$

Peak radiated power in a direction. TRP = EIRP averaged over sphere.

### Measurement methods

**Direct far-field:**
- DUT rotated on positioner
- Measurement antenna at far-field distance
- Full 3D sampling: 2° or 5° steps
- Time: 30-60 minutes per frequency

**Near-field to far-field transformation:**
- Probe scans spherical surface around DUT
- Mathematical transform to far-field pattern
- Faster for electrically large antennas
- Requires amplitude and phase measurement

**Multi-probe reverberation:**
- Multiple probe antennas surround DUT
- Statistical averaging over stirred modes
- Very fast: seconds per measurement
- Lower accuracy than direct methods

### 5G NR MIMO OTA specifics

**Beam-based metrics:**
- EIRP per beam
- Beam correspondence (TX/RX alignment)
- Beam switching time

**Throughput-based testing:**
- Measure actual data rate
- Channel emulator creates fading
- Multi-path, Doppler, MIMO conditions

### Positioner systems

| Type | Description | Use case |
|------|-------------|----------|
| 2-axis | φ, θ rotation | Standard TRP/TIS |
| 3-axis | Add head/hand phantom | Body effects |
| Multi-axis | 6-DOF robotic arm | Complex orientations |

<section class="lesson-apply">

## Apply

**Example: TRP measurement**

Smartphone at 2.4 GHz, 23 dBm conducted power.

Measure radiated power at 5° steps:
- 72 φ steps × 36 θ steps = 2592 points
- At each point: measure Pθ and Pφ

Integrate using conical section method:

$$\text{TRP} = \sum_{i,j} \frac{(P_{\theta,ij} + P_{\phi,ij}) \cdot \sin\theta_j \cdot \Delta\theta \cdot \Delta\phi}{4\pi}$$

Result: TRP = 19.5 dBm

Antenna efficiency: 19.5 - 23 = -3.5 dB (45%)

**Example: TIS measurement**

For each angular point, find minimum power for target BER (0.1% for voice, 10% for data).

If S(θ,φ) varies from -95 to -105 dBm:

$$\text{TIS} = \frac{4\pi}{\sum_{i,j} \frac{\sin\theta_j \Delta\theta \Delta\phi}{10^{S_{ij}/10}}}$$

**Example: 5G mmWave EIRP**

28 GHz phased array, 64 elements.

Per-element power: 10 dBm
Array gain: 18 dBi
EIRP = 10 + 18 = 28 dBm (at beam peak)

Regulatory limit: 43 dBm EIRP (FCC)
Margin: 15 dB — well below limit.

</section>

<section class="lesson-pitfalls">

## Pitfalls

1. **Forgetting polarization.** TRP requires both θ and φ polarizations. Single-polarization measurement misses half the power.

2. **Insufficient angular sampling.** Undersampling misses nulls and peaks. Use Nyquist rate for antenna electrical size.

3. **Ignoring body effects.** Hand/head proximity changes pattern dramatically. Test with standardized phantoms per CTIA.

4. **Wrong reference level.** TRP is total radiated. EIRP is directional. Don't confuse in link budgets.

5. **Temperature effects.** PA efficiency and antenna match change with temperature. Condition DUT before testing.

</section>

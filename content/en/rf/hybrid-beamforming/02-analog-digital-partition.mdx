<section class="lesson-hook">

## Why does partition matter?

The split between analog and digital processing determines your system's cost, power, flexibility, and performance. More RF chains = better spatial multiplexing but higher cost. Fewer RF chains = cheaper but reduced multi-user capability.

Getting this balance right is the core design decision in mmWave systems.

</section>

<section class="lesson-intuition">

## Intuition

Imagine a restaurant kitchen:
- **Analog side**: Prep cooks who can chop fast but only follow one recipe
- **Digital side**: Head chefs who can create anything but work slower and cost more

With unlimited budget, you'd hire all head chefs. In reality, you optimize the ratio based on menu complexity (number of streams) and dinner rush capacity (throughput requirements).

</section>

<section class="lesson-mechanism">

## Mechanism

### The fundamental constraint

$$N_s \leq N_{RF} \leq N_t$$

- $N_s$ = number of data streams
- $N_{RF}$ = number of RF chains
- $N_t$ = number of transmit antennas

More RF chains → closer to optimal digital beamforming performance.

### Cost model

| Component | Approximate cost |
|-----------|------------------|
| RF chain (28 GHz) | $300-800 |
| Phase shifter (4-bit) | $5-15 |
| Power amplifier | $50-200 |
| ADC (12-bit, 1 Gsps) | $100-500 |

For 64-element array:
- Fully digital: 64 RF chains ≈ $32,000
- Hybrid (8 RF chains): 8 RF chains + 64 PS ≈ $3,000

10× cost reduction with hybrid.

### Power consumption

| Component | Power per unit |
|-----------|---------------|
| RF chain | 200-500 mW |
| Phase shifter | 5-20 mW |
| ADC | 50-200 mW |
| DAC | 30-100 mW |

64-element array power:
- Fully digital: ~40W (RF chains + ADCs)
- Hybrid (8 chains): ~8W

5× power reduction.

### Performance vs. RF chains

Spectral efficiency vs. $N_{RF}$:

| $N_{RF}$ | % of optimal SE | Use case |
|----------|-----------------|----------|
| $N_s$ | 70-85% | Minimum viable |
| $2N_s$ | 90-95% | Good balance |
| $N_t$ | 100% | Fully digital (ideal) |

Rule of thumb: $N_{RF} = 2N_s$ captures most of the spatial multiplexing gain.

### Channel sparsity exploitation

mmWave channels have few dominant paths (typically 3-5 clusters).

Effective channel dimension: $\text{rank}(\mathbf{H}) \approx N_{clusters}$

If channel rank ≪ $N_t$, you don't need $N_t$ RF chains to capture all information.

### Sub-array sizing

For sub-array architecture with $N_t$ elements and $N_{RF}$ RF chains:

Sub-array size: $M = N_t / N_{RF}$

Sub-array beamwidth: $\theta_{3dB} \approx 102° / M$ (linear array)

Trade-off:
- Larger M → narrower beams, higher gain, less flexibility
- Smaller M → wider beams, lower gain, more beams simultaneously

</section>

<section class="lesson-apply">

## Apply

**Example: Base station design**

Requirements:
- 8 users simultaneously
- 64-element array
- Budget: $5,000 for RF front-end

Option A: $N_{RF} = 8$ (1 per user)
- Cost: 8 × $400 + 64 × $10 = $3,840
- SE: ~80% of optimal
- Within budget ✓

Option B: $N_{RF} = 16$ (2 per user)
- Cost: 16 × $400 + 64 × $10 = $7,040
- SE: ~92% of optimal
- Exceeds budget ✗

**Example: Sub-array beamwidth**

64-element ULA, $N_{RF} = 8$:
- Sub-array size: M = 64/8 = 8 elements
- Sub-array beamwidth: 102°/8 ≈ 13°
- Each sub-array can independently scan ±45°

**Example: Power budget allocation**

10W total RF power budget:
- 8 RF chains × 400 mW = 3.2W
- 64 phase shifters × 15 mW = 1.0W
- 8 ADCs × 150 mW = 1.2W
- 8 DACs × 75 mW = 0.6W
- Total: 6.0W → within budget with margin

**Example: Link budget impact**

Fully digital 64-element: 18 dBi per stream
Hybrid 8-sub-array: 12 dBi per sub-array

Link budget difference: 6 dB
- Compensate with 2× transmit power, or
- Accept 50% range reduction, or
- Use larger sub-arrays

</section>

<section class="lesson-pitfalls">

## Pitfalls

1. **Optimizing for wrong metric.** Cost, power, and performance all matter. A low-cost design that can't meet range requirements is useless.

2. **Ignoring channel statistics.** Indoor channels are richer than outdoor. Indoor may need more RF chains despite shorter range.

3. **Forgetting control overhead.** Each phase shifter needs control signals. 256 phase shifters × 6 bits = 1536 control lines.

4. **Assuming fixed partition.** Some architectures allow dynamic reconfiguration — merging sub-arrays for high-gain narrow beam or splitting for multi-user.

5. **Neglecting calibration.** Each RF chain needs calibration. More chains = more calibration complexity and drift compensation.

</section>

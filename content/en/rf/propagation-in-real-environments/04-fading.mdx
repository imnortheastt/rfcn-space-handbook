<section class="lesson-hook">

## Why understand fading?

Signal strength varies dramatically — 20-40 dB swings within half a wavelength. This isn't noise; it's multipath interference changing as you move. Understanding fading statistics (Rayleigh, Rician) and dynamics (Doppler, coherence time) lets you design systems that survive these fluctuations.

</section>

<section class="lesson-intuition">

## Intuition

Multiple signal paths arrive with different phases. As you move, path lengths change, phases shift, and the paths combine differently. Sometimes they add constructively (strong signal); sometimes destructively (deep fade). The faster you move, the faster the fading.

</section>

<section class="lesson-mechanism">

## Mechanism

### Small-scale fading distributions

**Rayleigh fading (no LOS):**

When many scattered paths, no dominant LOS:

$$f_R(r) = \frac{r}{\sigma^2} e^{-r^2/2\sigma^2}, \quad r \geq 0$$

Mean: $E[R] = \sigma\sqrt{\pi/2}$
Power: $E[R^2] = 2\sigma^2$

**Rician fading (with LOS):**

Dominant LOS path plus scattered paths:

$$f_R(r) = \frac{r}{\sigma^2} e^{-(r^2+s^2)/2\sigma^2} I_0\left(\frac{rs}{\sigma^2}\right)$$

where s = amplitude of LOS component, $I_0$ = modified Bessel function.

**K-factor:**
$$K = \frac{s^2}{2\sigma^2} = \frac{\text{LOS power}}{\text{scattered power}}$$

K = 0: Rayleigh
K → ∞: No fading (AWGN-like)
Typical: K = 3-10 dB for moderate LOS.

### Doppler effect

Moving at velocity v, carrier frequency f_c:

**Maximum Doppler shift:**
$$f_D = \frac{v}{\lambda} = \frac{vf_c}{c}$$

**Doppler spectrum (Jakes model):**

$$S(f) = \frac{1}{\pi f_D \sqrt{1 - (f/f_D)^2}}, \quad |f| < f_D$$

U-shaped spectrum with peaks at ±f_D.

### Coherence time

Time over which channel remains approximately constant:

$$T_c \approx \frac{0.423}{f_D} = \frac{0.423c}{vf_c}$$

Or: $T_c \approx \frac{1}{f_D}$ (order of magnitude).

### Fast vs. slow fading

| Condition | Type | Effect |
|-----------|------|--------|
| $T_s << T_c$ | Slow fading | Channel constant over symbol |
| $T_s >> T_c$ | Fast fading | Channel varies within symbol |

### Level crossing rate

Average number of times envelope crosses level R per second:

$$N_R = \sqrt{2\pi} f_D \rho e^{-\rho^2}$$

where $\rho = R/R_{rms}$.

### Average fade duration

Average time below threshold R:

$$\bar{\tau} = \frac{e^{\rho^2} - 1}{\rho \sqrt{2\pi} f_D}$$

</section>

<section class="lesson-apply">

## Apply

**Example: Vehicular at 60 mph**

Frequency: 2 GHz
Velocity: 60 mph ≈ 27 m/s

$$f_D = \frac{27 \times 2 \times 10^9}{3 \times 10^8} = 180 \text{ Hz}$$

Coherence time:
$$T_c = \frac{0.423}{180} = 2.4 \text{ ms}$$

LTE slot = 0.5 ms < 2.4 ms — slow fading per slot ✓.

**Example: Pedestrian**

Velocity: 1.5 m/s (walking), 900 MHz

$$f_D = \frac{1.5 \times 900 \times 10^6}{3 \times 10^8} = 4.5 \text{ Hz}$$

$$T_c = \frac{0.423}{4.5} = 94 \text{ ms}$$

Channel changes ~10× per second — slow enough for accurate CSI.

**Example: Deep fade probability**

Rayleigh fading. What's P(signal 20 dB below mean)?

20 dB below = $10^{-2}$ of mean power.

$$P(|h|^2 < 0.01) = 1 - e^{-0.01} \approx 1\%$$

~1% of time in 20 dB fade.

**Example: Fade duration**

180 Hz Doppler, 10 dB fade threshold (ρ = 0.316).

$$\bar{\tau} = \frac{e^{0.1} - 1}{0.316 \times \sqrt{2\pi} \times 180} = \frac{0.105}{142} = 0.74 \text{ ms}$$

Average 10 dB fade lasts 0.74 ms — ~100 bits at 128 kbps.

</section>

<section class="lesson-pitfalls">

## Pitfalls

1. **Assuming Rayleigh everywhere.** Indoor/urban may have LOS → Rician. Satellite almost always Rician.

2. **Ignoring Doppler at low speeds.** Even 5 m/s at 60 GHz gives 1 kHz Doppler — significant for mmWave.

3. **Confusing coherence time and delay spread.** Coherence time is temporal; coherence bandwidth is frequency. Different phenomena.

4. **Static channel assumption.** Channel estimation stales. Re-estimate at least every Tc.

5. **Underestimating fade depth.** Rayleigh fades can exceed 30 dB. Design margin for outage, not average.

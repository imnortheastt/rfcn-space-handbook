## Why this matters

Vietnam sits in ITU Region 3 (Asia-Pacific) and has its own national spectrum management framework administered by the Ministry of Information and Communications (MIC). For satellite operators serving Vietnamese users or engineers working on telecom projects in Vietnam, understanding this regulatory landscape—licensing requirements, band allocations, and coordination with VNPT and other operators—is essential for compliance and successful deployment.

<section class="lesson-pitfalls">

## Pitfalls

1. **Assuming US/EU rules apply.** Vietnam follows ITU Region 3 allocations with national variations.

2. **Underestimating process time.** Licensing can take 3-6 months; plan accordingly.

3. **Missing coordination letter.** Satellite operator authorization is mandatory; apply early.

4. **Import hurdles.** Satellite equipment may require separate import permits and face duties.

5. **Language barrier.** Most official documents are in Vietnamese; use authorized translators.

6. **Terrestrial interference.** Coordinate with cellular operators, especially in C-band.

7. **LEO uncertainty.** Starlink and other LEO services face evolving regulatory landscape.
